FACTS ABOUT BEES

Sweet, wholesome ideas to brighten your day.

Bees are more than just honey makers — they’re essential to life on Earth. These tiny creatures play a vital role in pollination, agriculture, and maintaining the balance of nature. Behind every jar of honey is a hardworking bee with amazing skills and behaviors worth discovering.

Bees can visit thousands of flowers each day

Worker bees tirelessly fly from flower to flower, collecting nectar and pollen. In doing so, they pollinate plants — a process that’s essential for the growth of fruits, vegetables, and even nuts.

They communicate by dancing

Honeybees perform a unique “waggle dance” inside the hive to tell others where to find rich sources of nectar. The direction and length of the dance give precise information about the location.

Making honey takes teamwork

It takes about 12 bees working their entire lives to produce just one teaspoon of honey. Each drop you enjoy represents hours of effort and cooperation inside the hive.

Bees have impressive memory and vision

Bees can recognize and remember human faces, thanks to their sharp vision and ability to process patterns — a rare skill in the insect world.

Bees are essential to our food supply

Nearly one-third of the food we eat depends on pollination by bees. Without them, many crops like apples, almonds, and blueberries would struggle to grow or disappear completely.
