HOW BEES MAKE HONEY

Sweet, wholesome ideas to brighten your day.

Honey isn’t just delicious — it’s also packed with antioxidants and natural healing properties. Here are five healthy recipes that let you enjoy the sweetness of honey while keeping things light and nutritious.

Honey production is one of nature’s most fascinating and efficient processes. It all starts when worker bees fly from flower to flower, collecting nectar using their long, straw-like tongues. This nectar is stored in a special part of their bodies called the “honey stomach,” which is different from their regular digestive system.

Once the bees return to the hive, they transfer the nectar to other worker bees by passing it mouth to mouth. During this process, natural enzymes are added, beginning a chemical transformation that breaks down the complex sugars in the nectar into simpler, more digestible ones.

Next, the processed nectar is carefully placed into the wax cells of the honeycomb. But it’s still too watery to become honey. So, bees fan their wings rapidly to create airflow that helps evaporate the excess moisture. Over time, the liquid thickens into the golden, syrupy honey we all know and love.

Finally, the bees seal each honey-filled cell with a thin layer of beeswax to keep it fresh and protected. This precious honey serves as food for the colony,  and when harvested responsibly, it becomes a delicious and healthful gift from nature to us.
